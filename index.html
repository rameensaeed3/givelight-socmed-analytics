Storytelling peaks, not posting volume — and Ramadan 2026 confirmed it.
More posts do not produce more views. The highest-reach months — October through December — ran at 9–18 posts and were carried by high-resonance content: the Palestinian Plate Art event, the 20-Year Gala, and the Mirchi Café launch. January's 34K content-level views look impressive, but a single post drove 12,620 of them — the story of Salma & Terry's Nikkah, where a wedding became a community fundraiser for Gaza orphans. Remove it and January is unremarkable.

March 2026 is the clearest proof. With 51 posts published — the most of any month — views collapsed to 12,284 and average reach fell to just 241 views per post. Every one of those 51 posts was Ramadan-related: iftar moments, orphan sponsorship appeals, and donation asks. This is GiveLight's most important fundraising window, yet it produced the weakest per-post reach of the year — likely a combination of Facebook penalizing high-frequency accounts and its algorithm suppressing donation-language content. Ramadan reached the already-converted and never broke through to new audiences.

The algorithm suppresses direct donation links — 82% fewer views
Posts linking directly to givelight.org/donate averaged just 214 views — compared to 1,191 views for posts without a donate link. That's an 82% reduction in reach purely from including the URL. Facebook's algorithm down-ranks content that drives users off-platform. The fix isn't to stop asking — it's to tell the story first, build emotional connection, and ask second.
Section 8

Key Findings & What To Do

01
Opportunity

Stories about people drive 4–10× more reach than fundraising asks

The Salma & Terry's Nikkah post (12,620 views) is GiveLight's single best performer of the year. It's a story about a couple, a wedding, a cause — not a donation pitch. The 20-Year Gala recap (8,191 views) follows the same pattern.

Action: Build a story bank. Every event, every host, every milestone gets a narrative-first post. Save donation asks for follow-ups within story comments or stories about specific children.

02
Risk

Posting frequency tripled in 2026, but reach per post collapsed

March 2026 published 51 posts and got just 14K views. December 2025 published 9 posts and got 26K views. The algorithm is suppressing high-volume, low-resonance accounts.

Action: Cut posting frequency to 3–4 high-quality posts per week. Quality over volume. Each post should answer: would I share this with a friend?

03
Warning

Ramadan 2026 underperformed — the prime fundraising window was missed

Ramadan content dominated the bottom 10 posts by interactions. The culprits: a daily Ramadan Hadith Series (templated religious quotes) and generic donation appeals — formats the audience consistently ignored even during the year's highest-giving window. Not a single Hadith series post earned more than 1 interaction.

Action: Replace templated series content with stories of specific children helped. Ramadan giving intent is high — the content needs to match it with emotional specificity, not generic appeals.

04
Risk

The audience is barely growing and skews mature

Net follow growth: just +63 in a full year (against 110 unfollows). Only 1.5% of followers are 18–24. In ten years, this base will start aging out.

Action: Build a Gen-Z and Millennial pipeline through GL Global Youth, university chapter content, and student-led events. The UCLA MSA post (rank 6 in bottom posts despite high engagement among those reached) shows latent demand — it just needs amplification.

05
Insight

Friday + Sunday + Afternoon is the posting sweet spot

Friday (1,339 avg views) and Sunday (1,031) outperform all other days. Saturday collapses to 308 — a 77% drop. By time of day, Afternoon (12–5pm) leads at 1,275 avg views; Night (9pm–5am) trails at 392.

Action: Prioritize Friday or Sunday afternoon for highest-impact posts. Avoid Saturday and late-night publishing.
